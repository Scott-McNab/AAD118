Project 01

Photography

Our first project was to photograph architecture of our choice using the methods of 'Macro' & 'Micro'.

Rule of thirds - this is a basic principle in photography, most cameras have the feature to put a grid on screen which breaks it up into 3 columns and 3 rows which gives a total of 9 squares:

As the image appears broken up it allows the you to focus on the 4 main points of interest which is ideally where the object in the photograph should be lined up to.


Fill the frame - this can make for a very interesting shot as there may be already so much happening with the object in the image any other object in the frame may take away from the shot entirely

Keep horizons, horizons - This is a very simple and obvious technique of just keeping any straight lines i.e the horizon, straight:

Project 02

Book Covers

For my second project of Data imaging and visualisation, I was asked to design a series of 3 book covers which had to be of the same genre and follow the same style so that they were consistent and felt like they actually belonged together.

From the start I knew what genre and books I wanted to do, I chose the harry Potter series seeing as I was such a huge fan when I was younger (and even now).

Other Book Covers

I started my research by looking at what covers already existed for the series of books and if there were any consistencies already discovered that i could perhaps bring through into my designs.

The first set of covers were from the books I had when I was a kid, and were most iconic to me. As these were books sparked my enthusiasm I decided I wanted to carry a part of them through to my design.

While looking for other variations I came across this series of covers, I took particular interest in theses covers as they displayed key parts of the story in a very clever way.


Design Inspiration


When looking for different I stumbled across a Graphic Designer who just went by the name ‘Carmen’, although she had designed a movie poster instead of a book cover I was still intrigued by the style that she had used. What interested me the most was the simplistic design to it, of just the title and a large image however they had been positioned in a way that complemented each other, this is what gave me the basis of my design.

I then came across a Designer called Rhiannon Edwards who studied at University of the West of England, Bristol. This is a poster that she designed for D&AD Student Awards in 2010, what I particularly like about this poster is that the main title is emphasised by it’s size and weight and how the subtitle is still there to be noticed for it’s importance however it has been made significantly smaller as it is less important but still holds enough importance to be placed just underneath as if it still has a relationship with the main title.

Also I like the way that the information on the poster has been placed just onto the white as it make it easy to read and would have not work as well on the green as it is a smaller font. I think the actual positioning of the text works very well and gives a strong sense of Swiss Style, which is the sort of style I want to carry on to my book covers.

Project 03

Infographic

The third and final project was to create an Infographic based on the World Population

First of I had to research what an infographic was.

A visual representation of information or data, e.g. as a chart or diagram.

To get a real feel of what an Infographic was and what way it should look I decided to look at a number of different ones which shared no relation to eachother as I thought this would help give me a better understanding.

Examples

I firstly decided to look up examples of World Population Infographics as this would th=be the topic I was doing mine on, so I thought it would be best what other infographics included in theirs and how they were displayed.My particular favourite is the one shown below as I think the information is easily displayed and easy to follow, I also like the use o colours and how it is easy to read the light text off the dark background.

As I am a big sport fan and particularly Football I decided to look at some football infographics, I found that they seemed a bit cluttered and didn't necessarily like any of the styles so I decided to focus more on individual players and who better to choose than the Pele. I like this particular infographic due to the lay out using the tiles to display the information and I also like the bright colours and how they used the colours which are the best fit for this particular infographic.

The third Infographic I decided to research was a gaming one, I came across some very appealing ones however I decided to look at a lot less appealing one as I found this one (although poorly designed) actually displayed the information most clearly and actually held some interesting elements.
